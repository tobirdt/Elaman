# Elaman design direction — Heritage Modern

## Status

Approved production direction for the current bilingual website.

## Experience statement

The site should feel like the carefully modernised homepage of an established German specialist company: direct, calm, discreet, and technically credible. It preserves the former site’s recognisable sequence and imagery while replacing dated presentation with better typography, responsive composition, and accessible interaction.

The objective is confidence, not conversion theatre. A visitor should understand who Elaman is, the breadth of its work, and how to make contact without encountering promotional pressure or operational detail.

## Visual character

- White and paper-soft are the default environment.
- Navy is reserved for the company overview and inquiry area.
- Elaman blue marks identity and active state.
- Red is concentrated in protection semantics and required form markers.
- Hairlines organise content locally without turning the page into a dashboard.
- Geist and Geist Mono provide a contemporary but neutral institutional voice.
- The supplied points-only Elaman signet stays visible in the compact header; the wordmark is reserved for metadata and brand collateral.

## Photography

The three supplied heritage images are the visual backbone:

1. Advice/chameleon macro — hero and company introduction
2. Tiger — transition into protection
3. Munich office — transition into contact

The imagery is static, tightly cropped, and never decorated with grid overlays, diagrams, or animated effects.

## Capability ledger direction

The portfolio is an editorial register, not a boxed feature grid:

- sequence number and capability name share one reading axis;
- horizontal rules create rhythm;
- vertical dividers and enclosed cells are absent;
- desktop uses two balanced columns;
- mobile uses one continuous column;
- labels wrap at phrase boundaries without automatic hyphenation.

This keeps the navy field authoritative and quiet while preserving easy scanning.

## Content tone

- Factual, concise, and human.
- Professional without bureaucratic filler.
- Discreet enough for the sector; no named clients or operational specifics.
- No invented claims, certifications, or statistics.
- German is composed as a primary language, not treated as a translation afterthought.
- Exactly one visible 20+ statement is enough.

## Motion character

Motion is nearly invisible:

- one progressive hero-copy entrance;
- short colour and rule feedback in navigation and controls;
- a direct mobile-menu state transition;
- no page-wide reveal choreography, scroll scrubbing, parallax, loops, or diagram animation.

Reduced-motion users receive the final state immediately.

## Responsive behavior

- Mobile: stacked hero, single-column ledger, stacked protection information, direct contact flow.
- Tablet: two-column ledger with protected reading measure and generous section spacing.
- Desktop: 50/50 hero, two-column ledger, two-column protection details, compact navigation.

All versions must retain the same information order, clear focus behavior, and zero horizontal overflow.

## Explicit anti-direction

Do not reintroduce an abandoned alternative through code or documentation. The current site has no Scroll Story, DotMatrix, signal map, technical grid, glass panels, floating cards, diagram system, or Framer Motion layer. It also has no place for cyberpunk imagery, product-dashboard language, a secure-portal claim, or sales-funnel urgency.
