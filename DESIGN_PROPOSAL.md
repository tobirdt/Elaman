# Elaman Redesign Exploration — Signal Spine

Status: proposed vertical-slice direction

Scope: Header/navigation, Hero, and ScrollStory only

Branch: `redesign-exploration`

## 1. Why the current site feels weak

The existing page is competent but visually undecided. It combines a large wordmark, a display headline, a technical-grid background, concentric circles, a glass diagram panel, three statistics, and two actions in the first viewport. None of those elements is individually dominant, so the hero reads as an accumulation of familiar security-site devices rather than a singular Elaman statement.

The same issue continues in the story. Desktop divides attention across three raised panels: introduction/progress, diagram, and active copy. The formation is a decorative bridge trace with several blue and red nodes, not a legible transformation of one system. Mobile becomes six conventional bordered cards with miniature diagrams and repeated disclosures. The result is structured but generic.

Four root causes:

1. **Composition:** visual weight is spread evenly instead of committed to one focal event.
2. **Hierarchy:** labels, diagrams, cards, badges, and statistics all ask for attention at similar intensity.
3. **Rhythm:** section pacing is generated by repeated containers and cards rather than content-specific tension and release.
4. **Motion and personality:** the hero has no authored entrance, while the story swaps panels and traces local paths. Motion does not yet express Elaman's core idea: scattered requirements becoming an integrated system.

Baseline evidence lives under `output/playwright/redesign-exploration/before/` for `/en` and `/de` at 1280×900 and 375×812.

## 2. Two viable directions

### Direction A — Signal Spine (selected)

Physical referent: a secure infrastructure binder opened across a bridge drawing, with a single blue registration spine connecting otherwise quiet sheets.

The interface uses one hard-edged Elaman-blue spatial event per viewport: a vertical or horizontal signal spine that joins word, dot state, and progression. Type and content sit on two distinct “banks.” A changing 25-dot formation crosses the span and becomes the proof of integration. Hairlines remain as functional bearings, not as a full dossier costume.

Why it wins:

- It is specific to the brand line “bridge to trust and security.”
- It gives the page a memorable architectural silhouette without new colours, fonts, imagery, or effects.
- It lets the dot mark remain the identity engine while avoiding dot wallpaper.
- It scales into a truthful lifecycle animation and a strong static mobile sequence.
- It preserves institutional restraint while feeling materially more authored than the current card-and-diagram language.

### Direction B — Blue Chamber (not selected)

Physical referent: a controlled communications room built from one uninterrupted blue plane and sparse white instrumentation.

The hero would be predominantly Elaman blue with white type and a large cut-out dot field. The story would return to white and use isolated full-screen chapters. This is more dramatic and immediately distinctive, but it makes the brand blue behave like a campaign colour, weakens the special role of the later navy protection band, and creates less continuity between hero and lifecycle. It also makes the one-blue-dot logic harder to preserve against a blue ground.

Direction B remains a future campaign option, not the stronger corporate-system choice.

## 3. Selected visual thesis

**Elaman connects separated technical domains through one controlled signal.**

The page should be remembered for a precise span between two fields, not for cards, grids, or decorative technology imagery. The span may be a blue plane, a rule, or a moving dot alignment, but every use must encode connection, progress, or active state.

The deliberate risk is scale and interruption: the hero headline becomes the principal visual object, and the signal spine visibly cuts through the composition instead of sitting inside a safe panel. Everything around that intervention stays quiet.

## 4. What stays and what changes

| Existing idea | Decision | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Elaman dot mark | Keep and formalise | It is the strongest ownable visual asset. Shared formation data replaces local SVG coordinate sets. |
| One blue point, red from protection onward | Keep | Scarcity gives both accents meaning. Hero and story steps 01–03 contain no red. |
| Hairlines | Keep selectively | Useful for bearings, progress, and chapter boundaries; not every object needs a bordered box. |
| Geist + Geist Mono | Reinstate | Fixed corporate constraint. Geist carries all reading; Mono is limited to state and sequence. |
| White-first canvas | Keep | Supports procurement-grade clarity and makes the blue spine decisive. |
| Technical-grid overlays | Remove | They imitate engineering without communicating anything and compete with the dot system. |
| Glass panels and broad shadows | Remove from the slice | They add generic depth and split the composition into unrelated cards. |
| Repeated tiny eyebrow treatment | Reduce | Labels remain only where they identify brand, state, or lifecycle sequence. |
| Three-column hero stats | Replace | They create the familiar hero-metric template and dilute the main statement. The verified 20+ fact moves into the narrative hierarchy rather than a sales strip. |
| Local bridge-path diagrams | Replace | The actual formation morph is more truthful and more specific to Elaman. |
| Sticky lifecycle | Keep, rebuild | It is the right interaction, but one shared progress value must drive formation, text, and progress. |
| Mobile story cards | Replace | Use an open linear ledger with chapter rules and static formation snapshots; no mini-card wall. |

## 5. Art direction and layout

### Header/navigation

- Sticky paper header with no backdrop blur.
- At page top, it feels almost unframed; after scroll, a graphite hairline and opaque paper state appear.
- Desktop: mark left, restrained navigation on one baseline, language and contact as compact controls.
- Active/hover state is a short blue line draw, never a pill background.
- Mobile: a real controlled disclosure with a 240–320ms transform/opacity transition, Escape and outside-click close, focus returned to the trigger, and a full-width paper overlay below the header.

### Hero

Desktop uses an asymmetric two-bank composition:

```text
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ mark / navigation                                          │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ELAMAN GMBH         │                                      │
│                     │        25-dot field                  │
│ Your bridge to      │        crossing one blue spine       │
│ trust and security. ├──────────────────────────────────────│
│                     │        factual system caption        │
│ intro + actions     │                                      │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- The H1 is the hero. Maximum desktop size is 96px with tracking no tighter than -0.04em.
- The wordmark is reduced to identification, not a second headline.
- One blue spine crosses the composition and terminates at the single blue formation point.
- No red appears in the hero.
- No stat strip, glass diagram, concentric circles, technical grid, or decorative gradient.
- Mobile collapses to one strong text column, then a shallow formation field. Actions remain stacked and at least 44px high.

### Formation Machine

Wide desktop becomes a single borderless sticky stage rather than three cards:

```text
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 02 / APPROACH                                              │
├───────────────┬───────────────────────┬────────────────────┤
│ six-step      │                       │ active chapter      │
│ ledger        │  shared 25-dot state  │ title               │
│               │  + one signal spine   │ description         │
│ 01 — 06       │                       │ key points           │
└───────────────┴───────────────────────┴────────────────────┘
```

- The same indexed 25 dots morph `field → columns → chain → perimeter → block → diamond`.
- One smoothed story MotionValue drives dot positions, active copy, and progress.
- Steps dwell long enough to read; the formation never becomes ambient animation.
- Red appears first at step 04 / Protection, remains contained at step 05, and resolves into the brand mark at step 06.
- Mobile/tablet/reduced motion uses six static formations in normal document flow, separated by chapter rules rather than cards.

## 6. Typography

| Role | Face | Proposed scale | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Hero display | Geist 650–700 | `clamp(3rem, 7.2vw, 6rem)` | 0.96–1.0 leading, -0.035em tracking, balanced wrapping |
| Story H2 | Geist 600–650 | `clamp(2.25rem, 4.4vw, 4rem)` | Strong contrast to body without mimicking hero |
| Chapter H3 | Geist 600 | `clamp(1.4rem, 2.1vw, 2rem)` | German-tested width |
| Lead | Geist 400–450 | `clamp(1.05rem, 1.35vw, 1.25rem)` | Maximum 65–70ch |
| Body | Geist 400 | 1rem | 1.6 leading |
| State/sequence | Geist Mono 500 | 0.6875–0.75rem | Uppercase only for terse metadata; 0.08–0.1em tracking |

No line break is hardcoded per locale. Width and scale must survive the longer German title at 375px, 768px, and short desktop heights.

## 7. Motion concept

Motion communicates assembly and orientation.

1. **Hero entrance, 600ms ceiling:** mark, label, headline, copy/actions, and formation enter in an authored sequence. Total stagger stays at or below 0.36s.
2. **Hero exit scrub:** the existing approved first scroll system moves only the formation field by approximately -16px and slightly reduces opacity.
3. **Header state, 180ms:** a threshold state introduces the hairline and paper surface; it is not a progress scrub.
4. **Story scrub:** the second and final scroll-linked system drives formation position, progress, and active chapter with the canonical scroll spring.
5. **State changes, 240–320ms:** menu and chapter text use opacity/translate only.
6. **Reduced motion:** final hero state is immediate; hero exit is static; desktop sticky story is replaced by the complete linear sequence; menu remains functional without spatial animation.

No width, height, top/left, blur, filter, font-size, or letter-spacing animation. Hairlines draw with scale transforms. No loops, bounce, particles, glow, or generic fade applied to every section.

## 8. Technical slice and boundaries

Expected implementation surface:

- `components/layout/Header.tsx`
- `components/layout/LanguageSwitcher.tsx` only if required for the new navigation contract
- `components/sections/HeroSection.tsx`
- `components/sections/ScrollStory.tsx`
- `components/sections/DesktopScrollStory.tsx`
- `components/sections/MobileStorySequence.tsx`
- affected `components/motion/` consumers
- a minimal set of shared formation/motion/UI primitives required by those three surfaces
- `components/layout/RootDocument.tsx` and typography tokens to restore Geist + Geist Mono

Explicitly untouched:

- all strings in `lib/content/site.ts`
- contact API and form logic
- routing, locale model, metadata paths, and legal content
- capabilities, systems, protection, delivery, contact, footer, and legal compositions
- dependencies and CI

The baseline branch does not currently contain the formation and motion modules named by the updated design documents. The slice may add those shared modules from the approved specification, but it must not use that as permission to roll the redesign across unrelated sections.

## 9. Acceptance criteria

- Header, hero, and story read as one authored visual thesis at 375, 768, 1280, and 1600px.
- EN and DE use identical component structure with no clipped or awkward display wrapping.
- The hero has one dominant statement and no generic diagram/card collage.
- The story visibly and truthfully transforms the same 25 dots through all six states.
- Exactly one blue dot is active in each formation; red first appears at step 04 with protection semantics.
- No new colours, fonts, dependencies, brand gradients, glass, grid overlay, or ordinary card shadow enter the slice.
- Keyboard, focus, touch targets, Escape/outside-click, reduced motion, and browser console are verified.
- `npm run lint`, `npm run typecheck`, and `npm run build` pass before sign-off.
